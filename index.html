Challenge 1 · Deliverable 2

3 Meta ad angles

Volley needs creative range, not one lucky ad. Three angles stress-test different buyers and objections — educational trust, transformation proof for premium skeptics, and humor for cold reach — each with hooks, full scripts, and shoot direction for Meta.

Educational Trust-first

Angle 01

Ali Abdaal energy · saves & shares

Audience

Self-improvers and professionals quietly curious about leveling up. Already consume creator content.

Hook type

Curiosity gap — "you're studying the wrong thing"

Core promise

"I'll make you smarter about how language learning actually works, then show you the tool."

Hook variations

"Most people fail language learning because they study the wrong thing." "One trick polyglots use that apps never teach you." "Learn 20% of a language correctly and you understand 80% of conversations."

Copy script · 30 sec

"Quick language tip most apps miss. You don't learn a language by memorizing words. Your brain learns through repetition, emotional context, and speaking pressure. That's why people spend two years on apps and still freeze when someone talks to them. Volley combines AI conversation practice, personalized audio lessons, and a real tutor who reviews your progress weekly. Your plan changes based on your weak spots. Try it if you actually want to speak the language, not collect streaks."

Visual direction

  • 9:16 vertical, 30–40 seconds
  • Selfie-cam educator, single take, clear room audio
  • B-roll inserts: app UI, conversation screen, tutor feedback
  • Style reference: Ali Abdaal short-form — soft teaching tone, not salesy
Social proof Transformation

Angle 02

Highest conviction for $99/mo

Audience

Adults who tried Duolingo or Babbel and quit. Skeptical of every app, especially premium ones.

Hook type

Specific outcome — career or life transformation

Core promise

"This actually worked for a normal person. Here's what changed."

Hook variations

"I closed my first international client last month. The crazy part? I learned Spanish in 4 months." "I proposed to my girlfriend in her native language after 4 months on this app." "The waiter gave us free dessert because I ordered in Italian. $99/month, worth every cent."

Copy script · 45 sec

"I run a small agency. Most prospects were US-based until last fall, when I started losing pitches to bilingual consultants. I'd tried Duolingo. Six months, nothing stuck. A friend recommended Volley. I was skeptical — $99 a month is real money. What's different is the structure. Ten minutes of AI conversation practice daily, audio lessons built for my weak spots, a tutor who reviews progress weekly. By month two I held basic calls. By month four I closed a $40k contract with a client in Mexico City. If you keep saying you'll learn a language someday, this is the closest thing I've found to a system that actually works for adults without time."

Visual direction

  • 9:16 vertical, 40–60 seconds
  • Real person, imperfect lighting — home office or kitchen
  • Proof artifacts on screen: WhatsApp messages, calendar streak, tutor notes
  • Style reference: founder testimonials — intentionally unpolished, not ad-agency
Humor / skit Reach play

Angle 03

Corporate Natalie · watch trial quality

[client visibly uncomfortable] Cut to narrator: "This is why professionals are quietly learning languages. With Volley, you practice real conversations daily using AI, while a tutor adjusts your plan every week. 10 minutes a day. Real speaking practice."

Visual direction

  • 9:16 vertical, 15–25 seconds
  • Fast skit, punchline-first, captions burned in
  • CTA card appears immediately after the emotional peak
  • Style reference: Corporate Natalie, Brian Jordan Alvarez

Challenge 2

Head of Sales · Snowball Agency

Cold DMs won't land A-players. This search targeted operators with media-native sales DNA — not org-chart picks from traditional SaaS.

Why low-effort cold DMs fail for A-players: The best operators in creator/media sales are already employed, well-networked, and allergic to generic recruiter copy. A templated "exciting opportunity" DM gets ignored because it signals you didn't do the work — and A-players optimize for who respects their time.

Why a 3 + 5 pipeline de-risks the hire: Top picks will be passive, slow to respond, or happily employed. Three primaries give optionality on culture fit and closing timeline; five alternates mean you never restart the search from zero when one drops — critical for a role that touches revenue from day one.
